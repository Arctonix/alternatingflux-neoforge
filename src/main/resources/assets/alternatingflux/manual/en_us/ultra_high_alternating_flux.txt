Ultra High Alternating Flux
When AF just isn't enough
Pushing flux modulation further yields §lUltra High Alternating Flux§r (UAF), a transmission tier above standard AF. It carries far greater current over the same long distances, at the cost of more demanding infrastructure. Like AF, UAF cannot directly power machines and must be stepped back down.<np>
UAF Wire is built atop the AF design, refining a finished AF coil with additional Constantan and Graphite to handle the higher load.<br><&wirecoil><np>
UAF Wires carry four times the current of AF lines over the same maximum length, with loss kept even lower than AF. Best reserved for trunk lines moving very large amounts of power across the map.<np>As with AF, a live UAF wire shocks any entity that touches it, and the larger loads these lines carry make that shock harsher still - keep well clear of energised UAF runs.<np>
<&relay>The §lUAF Relay§r is a taller relay post built like the AF Relay. As with AF, no machine connector exists - UAF is transmission-only.<np>
<&transformers>Two transformers bridge UAF to lower tiers: the brass §lHV–UAF Transformer§r steps between HV and UAF, and the salmon §lAF–UAF Transformer§r steps between AF and UAF. Both convert at a 1:1 rate.<np>
